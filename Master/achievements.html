About Our Basketball Achievements

2022 — NYP Takes the Championship

In 2022, Nanyang Polytechnic’s Basketball Team made history by winning the POL-ITE Games Men’s Basketball Championship for the first time in over a decade. With relentless training, tactical discipline, and sheer determination, our players overcame defending champions in a nail-biting final, cementing NYP as a serious contender in Singapore’s school basketball scene.

Key Highlights:

  • Defeated top-ranked Temasek Polytechnic in the finals
  • Average winning margin of 15 points throughout the tournament
  • MVP award secured by NYP point guard Daniel Lee
  • First NYP men’s team title since 2010

This victory reignited school spirit and set the foundation for the years to come.

2023 — Back-to-Back Glory

Building on the momentum from 2022, our team successfully defended the POL-ITE Men’s Championship in 2023. This year was not just about winning — it was about dominating. The squad showcased improved teamwork, sharper shooting accuracy, and a defensive wall that opponents struggled to break through.

Key Highlights:

  • Unbeaten record across the entire season
  • Top 3-point shooting percentage in the league
  • Captain Amir Rahman named “Best Defensive Player”
  • Introduced sports science programs to improve recovery

The 2023 win proved that NYP’s triumph was no fluke — it was the start of a basketball dynasty.

2024 — Triple Crown and National Recognition

2024 marked a new chapter — NYP not only clinched its third consecutive POL-ITE Men’s Championship, but also represented polytechnics at the Singapore University Games (SUNIG), finishing as national runners-up against university-level opponents.

Key Highlights:

  • Three consecutive POL-ITE titles (2022, 2023, 2024)
  • First NYP team to reach SUNIG finals
  • Four players scouted for Singapore’s U23 National Squad
  • Expanded training facilities with new shooting machines

From local dominance to national recognition, NYP’s basketball team has become a symbol of perseverance, talent, and school pride in Singapore.
