Dribbling Skillls

Dribbling training focuses on improving ball control, speed, and coordination in various game situations. Players work on using both their dominant and non-dominant hand, practicing moves like crossovers, behind-the-back dribbles, and spin moves to handle defensive pressure. Drills may involve weaving through cones, changing pace, and protecting the ball from defenders. This training helps players maintain control while moving at different speeds and angles, enabling them to break through defenses and set up plays effectively.

Shooting Practice

Shooting sessions aim to develop a player’s accuracy, consistency, and confidence when taking shots. Training starts with form shooting to reinforce proper technique, followed by mid-range and long-range shots, layups, and free throws. Players also practice shooting off the dribble, catching and shooting, and finishing under pressure. Game-like drills simulate defenders closing in, helping players adjust their timing and shot selection. This combination of repetition and situational practice ensures readiness to score in real matches.

Cardio & Conditioning

Cardio and conditioning are essential to maintaining peak performance throughout a game. Training often includes sprint intervals, agility ladder drills, and shuttle runs to improve speed and quickness. Endurance exercises such as continuous running, circuit workouts, and plyometric jumps build stamina and explosive power. These sessions also target overall fitness, ensuring players can sustain high-intensity play, recover quickly between plays, and remain effective in both offense and defense from start to finish.
